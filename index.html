Brief History of the Philippines

The Philippines is an archipelagic country that consists of 7,640 islands.
The country was colonized multiple times throught its history, and there will be a brief discussion of those times.

Spanish Era

The country was named after King Philip II of Spain (1556 - 1598), and was a colony of Spain for 333 years. During this time the Filipino people were treated unjustly and with cruelty by the friars.

In the late 19th century, a cry for nationalism arose, with Dr. Jose P. Rizal spearheading the revolution with his works Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo. Other notable people during the Spanish Revoltion are:

  • Andres Bonifacio, the founder of the Katipunan (KKK)

  • Emilio Aguinaldo, the first president of the Philippines

  • Apolinario Mabini, the brains of the revolution

The Spanish rule ended with the Spanish-American War, with the Americans taking over.

American Era

The American Period came in two phases. The first phase was from 1898 to 1935, when Washington described their mission as one to prepare the country for eventual independence.
The second period of United States rule was from 1936 to 1946, when the Commonwealth Government was established.

Manuel L. Quezon was the president during the Commonwealth Government, and then was succeeded by Sergio Osmena. Afterwards, the Japanese Occupation occured during World War II.

Japanese Occupation

The country was under a Puppet Government during the Japanese Occupation, with Jose P. Laurel as the President.
The occupation itself was being faught in secret by underground guerrilla groups such as the HUKBALAHAP or the Hukbong Bayan Laban sa Hapon.
